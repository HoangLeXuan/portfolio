Facial Beauty Prediction with Vision Transformer

This project builds a model using a Vision Transformer (ViT) to predict facial beauty. It uses transfer learning from ImageNet and compares the results with VGG-16 and ResNet-50 to see how well transformer-based models perform compared to traditional ones.

Objectives

During 2020, we experienced rise and advancement of Transformers in deep learning models being applied with both natural language processing and computer vision tasks. At the same time, CNN models were being implemented extensively across diverse real‑world applications, particularly in image classification, object detection, facial recognition, self‑driving cars, medical imaging, and robotic perception (Vaz et al., 2021), despite the proof that adapting Transformer architectures will help reduce computational cost and higher (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020).

So there are architects involving CNN models, but not a lot of problems have been solved using Vision Transformer, or ViT. Therefore, we want to evaluate when a task that is commonly solved with CNN, requires subtle attention to details, can be translated into ViT.

The study aimed to develop a Vision Transformer (ViT)-based architecture for Facial Beauty Prediction, leveraging transfer learning from a large-scale pre-trained model (ImageNet) and comparing its performance against two CNN baselines - VGG-16 and ResNet-50. The goal was to assess whether transformer-based models could outperform state-of-the-art CNNs in this task.

Data Preprocessing

We used the SCUT-FBP5500 dataset as the primary benchmark for this study. This dataset consists of 5,500 frontal facial images, with a gender distribution of 2,500 males and 3,000 females. The participants range in age from 15 to 60 years and include 2,000 Asian males, 2,000 Asian females, 750 Caucasian males, and 750 Caucasian females. Each image was evaluated by 60 independent raters using a 5-point scale, where 1 represented the lowest attractiveness score. The final score for each image was calculated as the mean of all ratings, minimizing individual bias and producing a more objective measure of perceived facial beauty.

We kept the original images quality and augmentation. We believe tasks involving subtle aesthetic evaluations requires the authenticity of the original facial features that are included in the dataset.

Process

  1. Reviewed literature on CNN-based models (VGG-16, ResNet-50) and the emerging Vision Transformer (ViT).
  2. Collected and prepared the SCUT-FBP5500 dataset (5,500 annotated facial images).
  3. Pre-processed data and applied transfer learning from ImageNet-pretrained models.
  4. Implemented and fine-tuned three architectures: VGG-16, ResNet-50, and Vision Transformer (ViT).
  5. Evaluated models using Mean Squared Error (MSE), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), and Pearson Correlation.
  6. Compared results to identify whether ViT outperforms state-of-the-art CNN baselines.

Value Proposition

This self-directed research project explores the application of Vision Transformer (ViT) models for facial beauty prediction, aiming to evaluate their effectiveness compared to traditional convolutional neural networks like VGG16 and ResNet-50. By leveraging pre-trained ViT architectures and fine-tuning them on benchmark datasets such as SCUT-FBP5500, the project seeks to gain hands-on experience with transformer-based models in computer vision and contribute to ongoing discussions in AI-powered aesthetic analysis.

What I Learned

  • Gained practical experience in applying transfer learning to improve model performance on limited datasets.
  • Understood the strengths and limitations of CNNs vs. Transformers for computer vision tasks.
  • Learned how ViTs capture global context more effectively than CNNs, which focus mainly on local features.
  • Improved skills in model evaluation using metrics such as MSE, MAE, and Pearson Correlation.
  • Hands-on experience with Python deep learning frameworks (TensorFlow/PyTorch) and GPU training.
  • Developed insights into how research can translate to real-world applications such as aesthetics, advertising, and social media.
