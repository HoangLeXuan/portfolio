Module 01

Ethical Principles Statement

Six commitments that govern every AI system IronBridge deploys — not aspirational buzzwords, but operational constraints with named owners and measurable outcomes.

Human Dignity

AI systems will never diminish the rights, safety, or dignity of workers, customers, or communities. Workers retain the right to contest any AI-influenced decision affecting their employment or safety.

Fairness & Non-Discrimination

We actively detect and mitigate bias across all AI models. Demographic parity ratio ≥ 0.80 and equalized odds difference < 0.05 are enforced production thresholds, not aspirational targets.

Transparency

Decision logic is explainable to affected parties. Mandatory SHAP/LIME explanations for Tier 1 decisions. Affected individuals may request explanations within 30 days.

Accountability

Named owners hold responsibility at every AI lifecycle stage. Harm incidents trigger root-cause analysis and remediation within 30 days. The AI Ethics Officer owns incident log governance.

Privacy & Security

Data minimization, AES-256 encryption at rest, and TLS 1.3 in transit protect all personal and proprietary data. GDPR/CCPA compliance is validated quarterly by Legal & Compliance.

Sustainability

AI deployment decisions weigh long-term environmental, social, and economic impacts. Annual impact reviews feed Board-level strategic AI planning, not just operational reporting.

Effective RAI Metrics

24 specific, measurable KPIs across 6 categories. Reviewed monthly by the Steering Committee. Thresholds are hard limits, not aspirational targets.

⚖ Fairness
  • Demographic parity ratio ≥ 0.80
  • Equalized odds difference < 0.05
  • Disparate impact ratio (80% rule)
  • Representation in training data by cohort
🔍 Transparency
  • % of Tier 1 decisions with explanation
  • Avg. explanation quality score (user survey)
  • Audit log completeness rate
  • Model card coverage of AI asset inventory
🔒 Privacy & Compliance
  • Data minimization compliance %
  • PII exposure incidents per quarter
  • GDPR/CCPA response time (target: <72 hrs)
  • Consent record coverage rate
📊 Reliability
  • Model accuracy drift threshold (±2%)
  • False positive/negative rates by cohort
  • Data quality score per pipeline
  • Mean time to detect distributional shift
📋 Governance
  • Training completion rate (target: 100%)
  • Incident resolution time (target: ≤30 days)
  • Risk assessment coverage of AI assets
  • Policy review cycle adherence
👁 Human Oversight
  • Override rate on AI recommendations
  • Human review coverage for Tier 1 AI
  • Avg. time to human review (hrs)
  • Reviewer agreement rate with AI output

Module 08

RAI Risk Evaluation Guidelines

Three critical risk domains with specific, actionable controls mapped to IronBridge's manufacturing context and regulatory environment.

Transparency & Explainability
  • Mandatory SHAP/LIME explanations for all Tier 1 decisions affecting workers or customers
  • Plain-language summaries required for non-technical stakeholders
  • Audit trail: model version + input → output retained for 7 years
  • Affected individuals can request explanation within 30 days (EU AI Act Art. 13)
Data Bias
  • Pre-training: stratified sampling; representation audit of training data
  • In-training: fairness constraints applied (IBM AI Fairness 360 toolkit)
  • Post-training: demographic parity ≥ 0.80; equalized odds < 0.05 as hard thresholds
  • Annual third-party bias audit for all Tier 1 & 2 AI systems; results published internally
Privacy, Protection & Compliance
  • GDPR Art. 22 compliance for automated decisions affecting individuals; CCPA opt-out required
  • Data minimization: collect only fields necessary for inference — validated quarterly
  • AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit; zero-trust access model
  • Breach response plan: notify supervisory authority within 72 hours; affected users within 30 days

Module 09

User Monitoring, Training & Education

Continuous monitoring catches automation bias and over-reliance before they become incidents. Ongoing education keeps IronBridge's workforce current on evolving AI risks.

User Monitoring
  • Real-time dashboard: AI recommendation acceptance rate, override rate, error flags — visible to Tier 2 managers and above
  • Monthly cohort analysis: identify users at risk of automation bias (override rate <5% triggers coaching)
  • Feedback loops: in-app "flag an issue" for AI outputs with 48-hour triage SLA
  • Quarterly user satisfaction survey on AI tools (target NPS ≥ 40)
  • Anomaly detection: alerts when override rate drops below 5% across a team (indicates possible over-reliance)
Ongoing Education
  • "AI Spotlight" monthly newsletter: real case studies, new tool updates, regulatory news in plain language
  • Role-based micro-modules (10–15 min): refreshed annually or within 60 days of any major incident
  • Lunch & Learns: quarterly sessions on emerging RAI topics (GenAI, LLMs, agentic AI)
  • Incident case studies: anonymized reviews shared org-wide to build RAI culture without blame
  • Curated external resources library: MIT Open RAI, NIST, Partnership on AI — updated quarterly

Module 10

Long-Term Impact Evaluation

IronBridge evaluates AI impacts across two primary stakeholder groups — workers and customers — with annual reviews feeding Board-level strategic planning.

👷 Employee Impacts

Job Displacement Risk
AI displaces routine tasks → mandatory reskilling program before deployment; 6-month transition support. Draws on Chen (2025) labor market displacement research.
Skill Evolution
Track workforce skill shift annually; partner with community colleges for retraining. Upskilling costs offset against projected productivity gains.
Worker Surveillance
AI-monitored performance: caps on data collected; union consultation required before any new monitoring AI is deployed.
Safety (Manufacturing Floor)
Human override required for all safety-critical decisions. Zero-tolerance for AI-only safety calls. Incident rate tracked against pre-AI baseline.

🛒 Customer Impacts

Algorithmic Discrimination
Annual disparate impact audit. Customers can contest AI-influenced decisions; human review within 5 business days. Results reported to Board.
Data Privacy
Explicit consent for personalization; data deletion on request within 30 days; annual privacy notice in plain language. GDPR/CCPA compliance validated quarterly.
Reliability of AI Outputs
SLA: AI-driven decisions carry ≥95% accuracy target; monthly validation against ground truth. Drift triggers immediate human review escalation.
Long-Run Brand Trust
Annual RAI transparency report published externally. Third-party audit results disclosed per Dell'Acqua et al. (2026) jagged frontier framework recommendations.

Module 11

Additional RAI Best Practices

Eight practices drawn from course learning, instructor feedback, and applied manufacturing context. These complement the policy framework with operational wisdom.

Cyborg-Centaur Hybrid Model
Balance full automation (Cyborg) with human-AI teaming (Centaur). Reserve full autonomy only for low-risk, easily reversible decisions.
Jagged Frontier Awareness
AI excels in some domains; fails unexpectedly in others. Map the frontier per use case — never assume AI capability transfers uniformly (Dell'Acqua et al., 2026).
Red-Team Before Deployment
Adversarial testing with diverse teams. Include manufacturing workers in QA — they surface failure modes engineers miss entirely.
Regulatory Horizon Scanning
Track EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF updates, and FTC AI guidance quarterly. Maintain a regulatory change log with assigned action items and owners.
Vendor RAI Due Diligence
All AI vendors must provide model cards, bias audit results, and data handling agreements before procurement approval. Non-negotiable contract clause.
Stakeholder Co-Design
Involve workers, customers, and community reps in AI design reviews — especially for high-impact manufacturing floor AI systems affecting safety or jobs.
Graceful Degradation
Every AI system must have a documented fallback to human process when AI fails or goes offline. Fallback tested in tabletop exercises annually.
Living Document Policy
RAI framework reviewed semi-annually; versioned and archived. All staff notified of material changes within 14 days. No silent updates.
