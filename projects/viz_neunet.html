04 — Loss Functions

Measuring the Error

Loss quantifies how wrong the model is. Training is the process of relentlessly driving this number toward zero — on data the model has never seen.

From the TF Playground screenshot

At epoch 1,980 the training loss reached 0.001 while the test loss sits at 0.075. This gap is the signature of overfitting — the network memorized some noise in the training set.

COMMON LOSS FUNCTIONS
Mean Squared Error
L = (1/n) Σ (y − ŷ)². Penalizes large errors heavily. Best for regression.
Binary Cross-Entropy
L = −[y log(ŷ) + (1−y)log(1−ŷ)]. Standard for binary classification.
Categorical Cross-Entropy
Extends BCE to multi-class problems. Used with softmax output.

Key Insights

Visualizing neural networks transforms abstract math into intuitive spatial understanding.

01
Depth enables abstraction
Each hidden layer detects increasingly complex patterns — from edges to shapes to objects. The spiral in TF Playground requires at least 2 hidden layers.
02
Weights are the memory
Everything the network "knows" lives in its weights. The thickness of connections in TF Playground directly encodes learned importance.
03
Non-linearity is essential
Without activation functions, any depth of network collapses to a single linear equation — incapable of modeling curves like the spiral boundary.
04
Loss gap signals overfitting
A training loss of 0.001 vs test loss of 0.075 means the model memorized noise. Regularization (L1 in the screenshot) helps close this gap.
05
Tanh maps to color
The orange/blue boundary in TF Playground is literally the Tanh output — orange for positive, blue for negative activations at each point in space.
06
Visualization accelerates learning
Seeing gradients flow, weights update, and decision boundaries sharpen turns hours of equation-reading into seconds of intuitive insight.
