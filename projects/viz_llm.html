Step 01 — Data Collection
🌐
Massive Data Acquisition
Scraping the web (Common Crawl), books, Wikipedia, code repositories, academic papers, and licensed datasets. GPT-4 trained on ~13 trillion tokens; LLaMA 3 on 15 trillion tokens. Quality filtering determines dataset value.
Terabytes → Petabytes of raw text
Step 02 — Preprocessing
🧹
Cleaning & Tokenization
Deduplication, PII removal, quality scoring, and language filtering. Text is converted into numeric tokens (GPT-4 uses ~100k token vocabulary). A single pre-training corpus can take weeks to process even on HPC clusters.
Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokenization
Step 03 — Architecture Design
🏗️
Transformer Architecture
All major LLMs use the Transformer architecture (attention mechanism). Engineers determine depth (layers), width (hidden dimensions), and number of attention heads. GPT-4 is speculated at ~1.8T parameters across mixture-of-experts.
Self-attention · Feed-forward layers · Embeddings
Step 04 — Pre-Training
⚡
Next-Token Prediction at Scale
The model learns to predict the next token across trillions of examples. Requires thousands of GPUs/TPUs running 24/7 for months. GPT-4 pre-training reportedly cost ~$100M; Gemini Ultra ~$191M in compute alone.
Cross-entropy loss · AdamW optimizer · Gradient checkpointing
Step 05 — Fine-Tuning (SFT)
🎯
Supervised Instruction Tuning
The base model is fine-tuned on curated instruction-response pairs written by human contractors. This teaches the model to follow directions, answer questions, and produce structured outputs. Costs: $1M–$10M in human labor.
~10k–100k high-quality human demonstrations
Step 06 — RLHF / Alignment
🛡️
Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback
Human raters rank model outputs; a reward model is trained on preferences. The LLM is then optimized against this reward model using PPO. Anthropic's Constitutional AI adds rule-based self-critique for safer alignment.
PPO · DPO · Constitutional AI · Reward modeling
Step 07 — Evaluation & Deployment
🚀
Red-Teaming, Benchmarks & Release
Models are evaluated on MMLU, HumanEval, MATH, and proprietary benchmarks. Safety red-teaming tests for harmful outputs. Deployment at scale requires inference clusters, often using quantization (FP8/INT4) to reduce memory costs.
Inference optimization · Model cards · API rollout
02

Resource Categories & Costs

🌐
Resource 01 — Data
Training Data
High-quality data is the foundation of LLM capability. Costs span web scraping infrastructure, licensed corpora (books, journals, code), and the human labor to clean and annotate it.
Common Crawl (free, raw) ~250TB/snapshot
Licensed book corpora $1M – $50M
Annotation / curation labor $1M – $10M
GPT-4 training tokens ~13 trillion
💻
Resource 02 — Compute
GPU / TPU Power
Training requires thousands of accelerators (NVIDIA A100/H100 or Google TPUs) networked together with high-bandwidth interconnects (NVLink, InfiniBand). Cloud compute is rented at $2–$8/GPU-hour.
GPT-4 (estimated) ~25,000 A100s
LLaMA 3 405B ~16,000 H100s
Gemini Ultra (est.) ~4,096+ TPU v4
Total compute cost (GPT-4 est.) ~$63M – $100M
⚡
Resource 03 — Energy
Power Consumption
Each GPU draws 300–700W. Datacenters require cooling, UPS systems, and redundant power. Training a single large frontier model can emit as much CO₂ as hundreds of transatlantic flights.
GPT-3 training CO₂ ~552 tonnes CO₂e
GPT-4 energy (estimated) ~50 GWh
Typical datacenter PUE 1.1 – 1.5×
Energy cost (at $0.08/kWh) ~$4M per run
⏱️
Resource 04 — Time
Duration & Human Labor
End-to-end LLM development spans 12–36 months per major release. Pre-training alone runs 2–6 months of continuous compute. Human researchers, engineers, and annotators represent a major fraction of total cost.
Pre-training duration 2 – 6 months
Full dev cycle (data → deploy) 12 – 36 months
Researcher team size 100 – 1,000+ FTE
Human labor cost (est.) $50M – $500M
